Program Description

The BreakerBots is a FIRST Robotics Competition (FRC) team from Pacific Grove, California. Coach Roby Hyde started the BreakerBots in 2014 with the goal of providing a welcoming space for students who shared an interest in STEM. Our team now has about 25 active students, with approximately 40% of them women. Students participate in more than just science; business, project management, communications, and art play a big role in the success of a team. As a team, we share the goal of learning valuable skills to carry into our future careers in science, technology, engineering, mathematics and beyond. FIRST robotics competition is a unique “varsity sport for the mind” designed to help high school students discover interesting and rewarding careers in engineering, science and technology. Teams are composed of students, professional engineers and other adult mentors who have just six weeks to work cooperatively to design and build a robot that can compete in a specifically designed robot game. FIRST provides students the ability to participate in real-world engineering challenges while learning time management, developing teamwork skills and building self-esteem.

The BreakerBots mission: To inspire students in STEM-related fields, foster teamwork, and provide a welcoming space for all who share our desire to pursue excellence.

For Inspiration and Recognition of Science and Technology (FIRST) is a non-profit organization founded by Dean Kamen in 1989 with the goal of inspiring young people to pursue an education in STEM-related fields. Through competition, cooperation, and gracious professionalism, FIRST reaches students throughout the United States and the world. You can find more information here.
